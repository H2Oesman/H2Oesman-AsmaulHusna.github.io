31. Al-Khabeer (الخبير) - The All-Aware, The Fully Aware

Source: myislam.org/99-names-of-allah/al-khabir/
الخَبِيرُ
Transliteration: Al-Khabeer
Listen to pronunciation of this Name

Meaning and Significance

Al-Khabeer is the One whose knowledge encompasses all things, both manifest and hidden. Nothing is hidden from His awareness, no matter how small or insignificant. He knows the reality of all things, their inner qualities, and their ultimate outcomes.

Ibn Kathir explains in his Tafsir that Al-Khabeer is the One who has thorough knowledge of the true nature of things, including what is in the hearts. His knowledge penetrates all veils and encompasses all matters, both major and minor.

Dalil (Evidence from Quran & Hadith)

Quranic Reference: Surah Al-An'am 6:103
لَا تُدْرِكُهُ الْأَبْصَارُ وَهُوَ يُدْرِكُ الْأَبْصَارَ وَهُوَ اللَّطِيفُ الْخَبِيرُ
Lā tudrikuhul-abṣāru wa huwa yudrikul-abṣār, wa huwa al-Laṭīful-Khabīr

"No vision can grasp Him, but His Grasp is over all vision. He is the Most Subtle, the All-Aware."

Explanation: This verse shows that while human vision cannot comprehend Allah, His knowledge encompasses all vision. Ibn Kathir explains that Allah's attribute of being Al-Khabeer here refers to His perfect knowledge of all things, including what the eyes cannot see.
Quranic Reference: Surah Al-Mulk 67:14
أَلَا يَعْلَمُ مَنْ خَلَقَ وَهُوَ اللَّطِيفُ الْخَبِيرُ
Alā ya'lamu man khalaqa wa huwa al-Laṭīful-Khabīr

"Does He who created not know, while He is the Most Subtle, the All-Aware?"

Explanation: This rhetorical question emphasizes that the Creator must necessarily have complete knowledge of His creation. Ibn Kathir comments that this verse refutes those who claim Allah doesn't know their deeds, affirming His perfect awareness.
